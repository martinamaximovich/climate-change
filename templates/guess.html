Temperature is a key metric for tracing the ongoing climate changes. Here, we're giving you a chance to make your guesses about 3 different temperature-related metrics that are all very useful in understanding climate change.
First, average min temperature is calculated by summing the daily minimum temperatures for an individual year and dividing it by the number of days in that year. Average max temperature is calculated in the same way but by using daily maximum temperatures. As the Earth warms overall, the winter is particularly vulnerable to rising temperatures. Overall, minimum temperatures have increased at a higher rate than average maximum temperatures, because much of the observed warming has taken place when and where it is usually the coldest: during winter, at night, and in the northern latitudes. To conveniently compare the changes in different areas with a single value, we use annual average temperature: this value is the average of the mean temperature for each day, where the daily mean equals to the average of daily maximum and minimum temperature.
